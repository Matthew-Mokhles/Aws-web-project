Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Aswan?

The best time to visit Aswan is from October to April when the weather is cooler and more pleasant. Winter temperatures range from 20-25°C (68-77°F), perfect for sightseeing.

How do I get to Abu Simbel from Aswan?

Abu Simbel is 280 km south of Aswan. You can take a flight (45 min), join a bus convoy (3-4 hours), or take a Lake Nasser cruise. Most tours depart very early in the morning.

Is Aswan safe for tourists?

Yes! Aswan is one of the safest cities in Egypt for tourists. The people are known for their warmth and hospitality. Standard travel precautions apply as with any destination.

Can I visit Nubian villages independently?

Yes, you can hire a motorboat from the Aswan corniche to the West Bank Nubian villages. Many villages welcome tourists and some families offer home-cooked meals and overnight stays.

What currency is used in Aswan?

The Egyptian Pound (EGP) is the official currency. ATMs are available in the city center. US Dollars and Euros are widely accepted at hotels and tourist shops, but local currency is recommended.

What is the Abu Simbel Sun Festival?

Twice a year (Feb 22 & Oct 22), sunlight illuminates the inner sanctum of Abu Simbel temple, lighting up the statues of Ramesses II. This remarkable event draws thousands of visitors annually.

What should I wear in Aswan?

Light, breathable clothing is recommended. When visiting temples and religious sites, modest dress (covering shoulders and knees) is respectful. Sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses are essential.

How do I subscribe to the newsletter?

Simply visit our homepage and enter your name and email in the subscription form. You'll receive a weekly newsletter with the latest Aswan news, travel tips, and cultural stories.

Can I take a Nile cruise from Aswan?

Absolutely! Aswan is the starting point for the famous Aswan-to-Luxor Nile cruise. The 3-4 night journey passes through Kom Ombo, Edfu, and offers an unforgettable experience on the Nile.
